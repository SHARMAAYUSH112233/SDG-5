What is Discrimination?

Discrimination in the context of prejudice is the process of making unfair or prejudicial distinctions between people based on the groups, classes, or other categories to which they belong or are perceived to belong, such as race, gender, age, religion, physical attractiveness, or sexual orientation.

What are stereotypes?

In social psychology, a stereotype is a generalized belief about a particular category of people. It is an expectation that people might have about every person of a particular group.

  • Girls and women are generally expected to dress in typically feminine ways and be polite, accommodating, and nurturing.
  • Men are generally expected to be strong, aggressive, and bold.
  • Many families expect to have boy and not a girl because they think that boys will help them and stay with them after the marriage.

What are the solutions for these problems?

There are various ways to address gender inequality. These include increasing women's representation in leadership and decision-making, redistributing care-work and productive resources, promoting diversity, equity, and inclusion within companies, supporting reproductive freedom, providing access to healthcare and sex education, and stopping child marriage and sexual harassment. Additionally, making education gender-sensitive and offering flexibility and empathy in the workplace are important steps towards reducing gender inequality.
